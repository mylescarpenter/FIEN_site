Unlock Potential, Transform Lives

Our mission is to enable needy students to achieve better education outcomes through tailored programs.

These programs include the Teachers Exchange Program (FTEP) for professional growth, the FIEN Health Insurance Program (FHIP) to provide healthcare access for needy students, and the Inclusive Education Program (IEP) to assist students with disabilities. We also offer the Digital Teaching Program (DTP), the Language Proficiency Program (LPP), and scholarships through the FIEN Scholars Program (FSP). Additionally, we provide research consultation through the FIEN Research Program (FRP), along with the Free Wi-Fi Program (FWP) and the FIENtech Program to promote learning and innovation among students.


The Foundation for Improving Education Outcomes of Needy Students (FIEN Foundation) is a non-governmental organization dedicated to providing needy students with the opportunity to enhance their learning experiences and academic achievements. By identifying and removing the barriers that hinder their educational success, FIEN aims to create a more equitable and supportive environment for students in need. The organization operates in Tanzania, Africa. The organisation was founded in 2015, and officially registered as a non-governmental organization in 2019, with the registration number 00NGO/R/0323.

Meet our founder

Magessa George earned his Bachelor of Education in Languages and Management from Mzumbe University in Tanzania, where he developed a strong foundation in both teaching and Management skills. He further advanced his academic journey by obtaining a Master of Science in Human Resource Management from the same institution, which enhanced his expertise in managing human resources effectively.

In addition to his formal education, Magessa George pursued leadership training at the University of Wisconsin-Madison in the United States, where he honed his leadership abilities and gained invaluable insights into effective organizational management.

Magessa began his professional career as a teacher in public schools in Morogoro Municipality in Tanzania, where he worked tirelessly to inspire and educate young minds. His passion for improving education and addressing the needs of underprivileged students led him to establish the Foundation for Improving Educational Outcomes for Needy Students (FIEN Foundation) in 2019. The foundation was born out of his desire to create lasting change in the education system and provide support to students who face barriers to succeed in their education.

In 2023, driven by his commitment to the mission of FIEN Foundation, Magessa George transitioned to a full-time role as the CEO, where he now leads the organization with dedication and vision, working to enhance educational opportunities to students across Tanzania.
